The Challenge with Traditional Shoeing

Restricted Movement

Traditional shoes lock the hoof in a rigid one-piece structure, preventing natural 3D movement

Poor Circulation

Limited heel expansion reduces blood pumping function and circulation

Increased Injury Risk

Up to 60% of lameness is related to osteoarthritis from altered biomechanics

What to Expect

Timeline of improvements with Twin™ Shoes

1-2 weeks

Immediate Effects

Horse adjusts to new proprioceptive input. Hoof capsule relaxation and toe gap opening often occurs within days.

3-12 weeks

Short Term

Significant improvement in gait quality, stride length, shoulder freedom, and reduced lameness. Recommend 4-week shoeing intervals.

12-34 weeks

Mid Term

Continuous improvement in gait quality, lameness reduction, and enhanced hoof quality, shape, and balance.

34+ weeks

Long Term

Most hoof health parameters stabilize. Toe gap typically stays under ¼ inch. Return to standard 6-week intervals.
